Overview

In the modern era of robotics, the primary sources of information are often images, depth data, and lidar, with a strong focus on semantic attributes such as object categories and types. However, the true potential of robotic foundation models may lie in exploring less conventional yet profoundly impactful dimensionsâ€”modalities, attributes, and reasoning that go beyond the usual semantic focus. This workshop explores these alternative focuses, including spatial reasoning, tactile feedback, affordance prediction, physical reasoning, and audio feedback. Spatial reasoning enables robots to navigate and interact with their environment more intuitively, understanding spatial relationships and object dynamics. Tactile feedback provides a sense of touch, allowing robots to perform tasks with precision and adaptability akin to human touch. Affordance prediction goes beyond mere object recognition to understanding potential uses and interactions, thereby enhancing a robot's decision-making capabilities. Physical reasoning involves recognizing potential object dynamics in a scene based on their geometric structure or physical composition. By highlighting these unseen dimensions, we aim to broaden the horizons of robotic foundation models, fostering innovation and pushing the boundaries of what robots can achieve in complex, real-world scenarios. Our workshop aims to unite researchers from diverse sub-communities, including robot learning, simulators, foundation models for robotic manipulation, embodied AI, perception, and sim2real, among others. The workshop will focus on two main agendas: (1) Highlight the latest existing efforts by featuring talks by researchers actively contributing in the related areas, and (2) Have an abstract/paper submission to hear from the community what they think are the unseen dimensions towards foundation models in robotics. This workshop will benefit the robot learning community by addressing current challenges in robotics tasks and identifying the missing elements needed to build a foundational robotics model. It will also spark discussions on integrating multimodality into these models.
